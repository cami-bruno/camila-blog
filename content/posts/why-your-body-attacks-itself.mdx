The immune system is supposed to be on your side. That's literally its whole job — recognize what's foreign, destroy it, protect you. And it does that, most of the time.

But sometimes it gets confused. It starts attacking your own cells like they're the enemy. That's what autoimmune diseases are. Lupus, multiple sclerosis, type 1 diabetes, celiac — over 80 conditions where your body is basically at war with itself.

What I can't stop thinking about is how fragile that makes us. The thing that protects you is the same thing that can destroy you, and the line between the two is apparently not that clear.

Also — women get autoimmune diseases way more than men. Like, nine times more in some cases. Nobody fully knows why.

We have 80+ of these diseases and we still don't really understand most of them. That's equal parts fascinating and terrifying to me.
